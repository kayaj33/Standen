02 — Client Approval

From draft to
published post.

The complete weekly cycle — Monday to Friday, every client, every week. Nothing sits waiting.

1
Monday — Pull client context
Open their Google Drive folder. Read voice guide. Note any industry news relevant this week.
2
Monday — Draft in Claude
"Write 3 LinkedIn posts for [Name], [Title]. Voice: [paste voice doc]. Topics: [topics]. Boldness: [1-5]." Edit every draft until it sounds like them.
3
Monday — Send for approval
Paste drafts into their Google Doc. Each post has a comment box. Email: "Please review by Wednesday — comment directly or reply OK to approve."
4
Wednesday — Client reviews in Google Doc
Client comments "approved" or leaves inline edits. All tracked in one place. No email chains. No version confusion.
5
Thursday — Schedule in Buffer
All 3 posts approved → Buffer → Post 1 Monday, Post 2 Wednesday, Post 3 Friday. Takes 10 minutes.
6
Posts go live automatically
Buffer publishes. You don't touch anything. Client sees their post. The system runs itself.
Why Google Docs — Not Email

Email creates versions. Docs create one living source of truth. The client's edits are visible in real time. The entire approval history lives in one place — no searching threads for "did they approve post 2?"
