01 — Introduction

Let's start with
who you actually are.

Not just the title on your LinkedIn. How you'd introduce yourself to someone whose respect you want to earn in the first sixty seconds.

02 — Positioning

What do you want
to be known for?

Not your job description. Not your company's mission. The specific perspective or philosophy that is uniquely yours — the thing a colleague would mention first if someone asked what makes you exceptional.

03 — Perspective

What does everyone
in your field get wrong?

Every genuine expert has a perspective the mainstream gets wrong. The conventional wisdom they quietly disagree with. The approach that frustrates them. This is often where your most powerful content lives — and what separates a thought leader from a content producer.

04 — Industry Intelligence

What is most
misunderstood right now?

You have a front-row seat to what's actually happening in regulated industries. What are the people in boardrooms and compliance offices genuinely grappling with — that the public narrative is getting wrong or underrepresenting?

05 — Goals

What are you
building toward?

Your public presence should be working toward something specific. These answers shape the entire strategic direction of your content — they are confidential and essential.

06 — Audience

Who specifically
needs to hear you?

Not a broad category. The specific person who, if they read your posts consistently for ninety days, would make a decision or reach out in a way that meaningfully changes your trajectory.

07 — Voice

How do you actually
communicate?

Your content needs to sound like you — not a polished version of you. We need to understand how you actually speak, think out loud, and explain things to someone you respect. And equally important: what would make you wince if you saw it under your name.

08 — Risk & Boldness

How bold should
your voice be?

There is a spectrum between carefully measured thought leadership and genuinely provocative positioning. Both build audiences — they attract different people and carry different risk profiles. Where do you sit, honestly?

09 — Reference Points

Whose voice
do you admire?

Think of people — in your industry or outside it — whose LinkedIn presence, writing, or public thinking resonates with you. Not because they're famous, but because something about how they communicate feels right. This tells us more about your ideal voice than any description could.

10 — The Last Thing

What haven't
we asked yet?

The thing you were hoping we'd ask about. The context that makes everything else make more sense. The career moment, the professional philosophy, the thing that would let us write for you with genuine precision rather than approximation.
